FTC Endorsement and Testimonial Guidelines for Pharmaceuticals
=============================================================

1. General Disclosures
Any endorsement or testimonial must reflect the honest opinions, findings, beliefs, or experience of the endorser. If there is a "material connection" between the endorser (e.g., an influencer, a doctor, or a patient) and the pharmaceutical company, that connection must be clearly and conspicuously disclosed in the advertisement.

2. Patient Testimonials
If a patient provides a testimonial about a specific drug, the advertisement must not convey that the patient's experience is typical unless the company has adequate substantiation that the results are generally expected for the average patient. If the results are not typical, a clear disclaimer must be displayed (e.g., "Results not typical. Your results may vary."). 

3. Physician Endorsements
When an advertisement represents that the endorser is a doctor or medical expert, their qualifications must be genuine, and their endorsement must be supported by an actual evaluation or examination of the product that is consistent with the standard of medical care. The physician cannot make efficacy or safety claims that exceed the drug's FDA-approved label.

4. Video Advertising Specifics
In video formats, including TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram Reels, disclosures regarding material connections (such as "Paid Partnership" or "Sponsored") must be superimposed directly on the video itself in a font size and color that contrasts with the background. It must remain on screen long enough to be read. Burying the disclosure in the video description or in a hashtag cluster is insufficient.
